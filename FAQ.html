Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take my ceramic home with me when I leave, or do I need to come back and pick it up?

The majority of our ceramics are painted using acrylic paints. When you are finished and the paint is dry, we will spray it with a glossy glaze or a matte glaze. It takes about 5 minutes to dry, then you can take it home with you.

Ceramic dishes that you plan to eat off of, will need to be painted with special glazes and/or underglazes, so they will be food safe. These ceramics will need to be re-fired, so you will leave them here when you leave and I will contact you as soon as they are ready to be picked up. It shouldn't take longer than a week.

Do you make these ceramics yourself? Did you make the molds?

I did not make the molds, but I do make the ceramics myself. It's a long process to get each ceramic. First, I put rubber bands around the mold, to hold it tightly closed, then I fill the mold with casting slip, let it sit for 5 to 10 minutes (depending on the type and size of each ceramic), then I pour the slip out. The ceramic is then left in the mold to dry for so many hours (again it depends on the size and style of ceramic). When it is dry enough, I gently take it out of the mold and set it on the table to dry. If it needs to have holes added, different parts of it cut out, or pieces that were poured separately and need to be put together, I will do that at this point. I let the ceramic sit and dry for a couple of days (It needs to be completely dry or it will blow up and break into pieces in the kiln). When the ceramic is dry, I use my scrapping tools to smooth out the edges that were made from the mold. This is the point where I put the ceramics in the kiln to be fired. They sit in the kiln for 24 hours after I start the kiln going. Getting the ceramics ready to paint is a long process, but it is so worth it and so exciting to see the end result.

Can I chose my ceramic before I come?

I don't have a catalog set up yet. I have over 600 molds and I have not been able to pour all of them yet. I have pictures of all of the ceramics I have poured plus pictures of most of the molds. I'm working on getting these in a catalog so people can look through and chose in advance, but I'm not sure how long that will take me. Until then, you are welcome to come over and I will let you look through the pictures that I have.
